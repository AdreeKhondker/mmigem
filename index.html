What Are We Doing?

In our project, we are engineering bacteria that will train the human body to sense and target proliferating prostate cancer cells. The general pathway will allow for bacterial cell engulftion by dendritic cells (DC) and lead to presentation of prostate cancer specific antigens on the surface of the DC, while also inserting a near-infrared fluorescent protein gene. As the DC recognize the prostate cancer cell, a respective signal transduction cascade will up-regulate fluorescent protein expression. Using a novel engineered sensor, we will be able to measure cancer proliferation and DC engulftion of the prostate cancer cells. Overall, our project will lead to a stronger detection and treatment system for prostate cancer.
